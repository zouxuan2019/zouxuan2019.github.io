Share

Keeping one of the settings on Auto in most light varying environment

In most non-studio environments, we do not have control over the lighting conditions. It can be anything from passing clouds to walking through a museum where each room is lit differently.
Running all 3 settings on manual may leave you scrambling to balance your exposure.
Keeping one of the settings on Auto is a quick workaround.

  • In order to achieve 1 Auto, you may refer to the following guidelines
    • Auto Aperture: “S” Mode + Maunal ISO
    • Auto Speed: “A” Mode + Manual ISO
    • Auto ISO: “M” Mode + Auto ISO
  • The last one is my personal favourite,it helps rejecting unintended blur images caused by a slow shutter speed. That leaves ISO to do the heavy lifting.

Shade

  • Shade can be your best friend.
  • If there is no way you can make the available light work for your photo, shoot in the shade.
  • You’ll get a nice even exposure with no patchy highlights throughout your shot.

Limit your palette

  • When photos have too many colours spewing out from them, they’re often hard to look at.
  • Unless it’s a photo of a rainbow or the Mardi Gras.
  • Try to focus on having one or two colours predominately featuring in your photograph.
  • It will be more pleasing to the eye and will help set the tone of the image.
