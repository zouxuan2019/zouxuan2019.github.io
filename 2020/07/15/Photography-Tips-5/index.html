Photography Tips - 5

Share

How to Blur the background in Portraits

  • Use a large aperture, say, in the range of f/2 to f/4.
  • Increase the distance between your subject and the background.
  • Decrease the distance between you and the subject.
  • Use a longer focal length lens.

Try Panning

  • Select a shutter speed that’s slightly slower than you’d normally use - perhaps by one or two stops. For example, if you’d normally shoot at 1/125 seconds, lower it to 1/30 seconds.
  • As the subject moves in front of the camera, move the camera along with it, maintaining the subject in the frame. As you do, keep your finger on the shutter button, pressing it down halfway to lock the focus.
  • Press the shutter all the way down, ensuring that you continue the panning motion, even after the shutter has fired.
  • More details watch videos from here https://youtu.be/lnpc6QsoKo4

Sunny 16 Rule

  • On a sunny day, set your aperture to f/16, and then set the shutter speed and ISO values to the inverse of one another. So, if the ISO is set to 200, the shutter speed would be 1/200 seconds. If the ISO is set to 100, the shutter speed would be 1/100 seconds.
  • F16(Sunny)->F11(Cloudy)->F8(Overcast)->F5.6(Heavy Overcast)->F4(Sunset)->F2.8->F2->F1.4->F1 The F setting doubles the light than the one in front.
  • If you keep ISO constant, say 100, in order to get same exposure,increase aperture from F16 to F11 will lead to a double faster shutter speed, namely from 1/100 to 1/200
  • More details refer to :https://www.photographytalk.com/beginner-photography-tips/understanding-the-sunny-16-rule
