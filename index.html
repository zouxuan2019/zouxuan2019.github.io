Best Practices for Testing in Kotlin

Share

Junit5

  • Use @TestInstance(Lifecycle.PER_CLASS) to avoid the need for static members, which are non-idiomatic and cumbersome in Kotlin.

  • Instead of annotating every class with @TestInstance() you can change the default lifecycle with the junit-platform.properties file.

Naming, Grouping

  • Put the test method names in backticks and use spaces.

  • Use @Nested inner classes to group the test methods.

Libraries

  • There are so many test libraries. Check them out and make up your own mind.
    Language Test Frameworks Mocking Assertions
    Kotlin Spek
    KotlinTest    		 Mockito-Kotlin
    MockK    		 Strikt
    Atrium
    HamKrest
    Expekt
    Kluent/AssertK
    Java Junit5 AssertJ

Mock Handling

  • Don’t recreate, reset! Create mocks only once and reset them in a @BeforeEach. This way, you can use val fields and achieve better performance.
  • Use MockK to create mocks in a convenient and idiomatic way. It can also mock final classes by default.

Data Classes

  • Create a reference object and compare it directly with the actual object using an equality assertion.

    • Single Element
    • Lists

  • Use data classes to carry the test data (input and expected output) in a @ParameterizedTest.

Helper Functions

  • Write helper functions with default arguments to easily create instances with a complex structure.
    • Helper Function Example
    • Usage
  • Helper extension functions are useful to concisely create frequently used values (like Instant, UUID)
  • Test-specific extension functions are useful to extend libraries in an idiomatic way to remove distractions in the test code.

How to Go from Being Merely Good to Being Truly Great

  • The secret to maximizing your odds of success is to choose the right field of competition. (Know your strength)
  • Play a game that favors your strengths. If you can’t find a game that favors you, create one.
  • Habits are easier when they align with your natural abilities. Choose the habits that best suit you.
  • Pick the right habit and progress is easy. Pick the wrong habit and life is a struggle.
  • Genes cannot be easily changed, which means they provide a powerful advantage in favorable circumstances and a serious disadvantage in unfavorable circumstances.
  • Genes do not eliminate the need for hard work. They clarify it. They tell us what to work hard on.

How to Stay Motivated in Life and Work

  • The Goldilocks Rule states that humans experience peak motivation when working on tasks that are right on the edge of their current abilities.
  • The greatest threat to success is not failure but boredom.
  • As habits become routine, they become less interesting and less satisfying. We get bored.
  • Anyone can work hard when they feel motivated. It’s the ability to keep going when work isn’t exciting that makes the difference.
  • Professionals stick to the schedule; amateurs let life get in the way.

The Downside of Creating Good Habits

  • The upside of habits is that we can do things without thinking.
    The downside is that we stop paying attention to little errors.
  • Habits + Deliberate Practice = Mastery
  • Reflection and review is a process that allows you to remain conscious of your performance over time.
    • Perform an Annual Review,Reflect on the previous year
      • Count up how many books you have read
      • How many workouts you put in
      • How many new places you visited
      • more…
    • Reflect on progress
      • What went well this year?
      • What didn’t go so well this year?
      • What did I learn?
    • Six months after Reflection, conduct an Integrity Report
      • What are the core values that drive my life and work?
      • How am I living and working with integrity right now?
      • How can I set a higher standard in the future?
  • The tighter we cling to an identity, the harder it becomes to grow beyond it.

Conclusion： The Secret to Results That Last

  • The secret to getting results that last is to never stop making improvements.
  • It’s remarkable what you can build if you just don’t stop.
    • It’s remarkable the business you can build if you don’t stop working.
    • It’s remarkable the body you can build if you don’t stop training
    • It’s remarkable the knowledge you can build if you don’t stop learning
    • It’s remarkable the fortune you can build if you don’t stop saving
    • It’s remarkable the friendships you can build if you don’t stop caring
  • Small habits don’t add up. They compound.That’s the power of atomic habits. Tiny changes. Remarkable results.

HOW TO BREAK A BAD HABIT

Make It Invisible

  • Reduce exposure. Remove the cues of your bad habits from your environment.
    If you can’t seem to get any work done, leave your phone in another room for a few hours.

Make It Unattractive

  • Reframe your mindset. Highlight the benefits of avoiding your bad habits.
    Exercise. Many people associate exercise with being a challenging task that drains energy and wears you down. You can just as easily view it as a way to develop skills and build you up. Instead of telling yourself “I need to go run in the morning,” say “It’s time to build endurance and get fast.”

Make It Difficult

  • Increase friction. Increase the number of steps between you and your bad habits.
    If you’re playing too many video games, unplug the console and put it in a closet after each use.

  • Use a commitment device. Restrict your future choices to the ones that benefit you.
    A commitment device is a choice you make in the present that controls your actions in the future.
    You can reduce overeating by purchasing food in individual packages rather than in bulk size.
    If you’re excited about the business you want to start, email an entrepreneur you respect and set up a consulting call. When the time comes to act, the only way to bail is to cancel the meeting,whichrequires effort and may cost money.

Make It Unsatisfying

  • Get an accountability partner. Ask someone to watch your behavior.

  • Create a habit contract. Make the costs of your bad habits public and painful.

How to create a good habit - Part 2

Make it easy

  • Reduce friction. Decrease the number of steps between you and your good habits.
    Want to exercise? Set out your workout clothes, shoes, gym bag,and water bottle ahead of time.
  • Prime the environment. Prepare your environment to make future actions easier.
    Prepare healthy breakfast ingredients the nigh before.When I wake up, making breakfast will be easy.
  • Master the decisive moment. Optimize the small choices that deliver outsized impact.
    Walking into a restaurant is a decisive moment because it determines what you’ll be eating for lunch.Technically, you are in control of what you order, but in a larger sense, you can only order an item if it is on the menu.
  • Use the Two-Minute Rule. Downscale your habits until they can be done in two minutes or less.
    “Read before bed each night” becomes “Read one page.”
    For myself: "Write a blog for 5 mins at 10:55pm, but later I found I couldn't stop after 5 mins"
  • Automate your habits. Invest in technology and onetime purchases that lock in future behavior.
    Personal finance: Set an auto sehedule to transfer from your current deposit account to a fixed term deposit account each month
    Cut off social medial browsing with Screen Time

Make it Satisfying

  • Use reinforcement. Give yourself an immediate reward when you complete your habit.
    For instance: Allow myself to watch a short movie video after exercising for half an hour
  • Make “doing nothing” enjoyable. When avoiding a bad
    habit, design a way to see the benefits.
    For instance for smoking: You think you are quitting something, but you’re not quitting anything because cigarettes do nothing for you.You think smoking is something you need to do to be social, but it’s not. You can be social without smoking at all. You think smoking is about relieving stress, but it’s not. Smoking does not relieve your nerves, it destroys them.
  • Use a habit tracker. Keep track of your habit streak and
    “don’t break the chain.”
    For instance: Note down my exercise date on a calendar
  • Never miss twice. When you forget to do a habit, make sure
    you get back on track immediately.
    For instance: Whenever there is one empty mark, make sure it won't make two in a row.

How to create a good habit - Part 1

Make it obvious

  • Write down you current habits to become aware of them

  • Use implementation intentions: “ I will [BEHABIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCAITON].
    I will write a blog at 10:55 pm in my bedroom.

  • Use habit stacking: “After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]”.
    After getting off work, I will clean up my room.

  • Design your environpment. Make the cues of good habits obvious and visible.
    I will keep a notebook and a pen on my desk, which reminds me to write more happy moments.

Make it Attractive

  • Use temptation bundling. Pair an action you want to do with an action you need to do.
    After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [HABIT I NEED].
    After [HABIT I NEED], I will [HABIT I WANT].
    1.After preparing my breakfast, I will say one thing I'm grateful for that happened yesterday (need).
    2.After I say one thing I'm grateful for, I will eat my breakfast (want).

  • Join a culture where your desired behavior is the normal behavior.
    I will play tennis with my colleagues on weekends and discuss tennis skills in wechat group.

  • Create a motivation ritual. Do something you enjoy immediately before a difficult habit.
    Always play the same music (卡农) before start working on my school project. Then I'll begin to link the music with the act. Whenver I need to get in the mood, just press play.

MockK - 1

Share

Dependency

  • Maven
  • Gradle

Integrate with Junit 5

  • Add @ExtendWith(MockKExtension::class) on top of your test class

Annoations

  • @MockK: Creates mocks that are strict, so you need to provide some behaviour.
  • @MockK(relaxUnitFun = true): Make Unit returning functions to be relaxed
  • @RelaxedMockK: A relaxed mock is the mock that returns some simple value for all functions. This allows to skip specifying behavior for each case, while still allowing to stub things you need
  • @SpyK: Spies allow to mix mocks and real objects.
    Note: the spy object is a copy of a passed object.
  • @InjectMockKs: @InjectMockKs by default is injecting only lateinit vars or vars that are not assigned. To change this, use overrideValues = true. This would assign the value even if it is already somehow initialized. To inject vals, use injectImmutable = true. For a shorter notation use @OverrideMockKs which does the same as @InjectMockKs by default, but turns these two flags on.
  • Injection first tries to match properties by name, then by class or superclass.
  • Properties are injected even if private is applied. Constructors for injection are selected from the biggest number of arguments to lowest.
