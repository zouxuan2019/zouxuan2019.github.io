The Law of the Garbage Truck

Share

Do let Garbage Trucks pass you by

1) You do not have to be burdened by negative things you cannot control.
2) You are free to focus on what matters.

Do let own Garbage Trucks pass you by

Bad memories of your past do not have to weigh you down, and you do not have to be limited by your own negative projections of the future.

Do avoid becoming someone else’s Garbage Truck

1) Your response to people who dump on you does not have to be vengeful and judgmental.
2) Instead, you can give people a chance, stop baiting them, and be more forgiving.

Do help to Garbage Trucks you can

1) Stop dumping people on social network
2) You acknowledge and celebrate the best in others. You seek opportunities to communicate effectively with everyone.

Do honor your no Garbage Trucks! Pledge

1) You do not have to accept garbage and you do not need to spread it.
2) You are not a Garbage Truck.

Do live in the gratitude cycle and live free of the garbage cycle

1) Invest your energy in living a good meaningful life.
2) By choosing gratitude, not garbage, you help make the world a better place for everyone.

Do Live in a no Garbage Trucks! Zone

1) Let everyone know you are committed to living in the Gratitude Cycle and outside the grip of Garbage Trucks.
2) People will know where you stand: YOU LIVE IN A NO GARBAGE TRUCK! ZONE

Do Work in a No Garbage Trucks! Zone

By giving your best and expecting the best from others, you contribution an environment that allows people to enjoy their work, use their strengths, and feel what they do is meaningful.

The 8 commitments/lwas that will change your life forever.

If there be righteousness in the heart, there will be beauty in the character.
If there be beauty in the character, there will be harmony in the home.
If there be harmony in the home, there will be order in the nation
If there be order in the nation, there will be peace in the world.

The Inner Game of Tennis by W.Timothy Gallwey

Share

Inner Game vs outer Game

1) Every game is composed of two parts, an outer game and an inner game
2) The outer game is played against opponents and is filled with lots of contradictory advice.
3) The inner game is played within the player’s mind and its’ principal obstacles are self-doubt & anxiety.
4) Mastering the inner game is knowing how to get out of your own way to let your best game emerge.
5) Mastering the inner game is learning to overcome the self-doubt, nervousness, anxiety & lapses of concentration that keep you from performing at your best.

How to play our best

1) Each player has 2 “selves”. Self 1 is ego-mind or “teller” (“Hit the ball like this”) & Self 2 is natural ability or “doer” (the actual movement of the muscles to hit the ball).
2) To play our best, we must quiet Self 1 and let Self 2 do what it knows how to do. Self 2 likes images and pictures.
3) The best way to quiet the mind is not to tell it to shut up, or argue with it or criticize it for criticizing you. What works best is learning to focus it.
4) Focus means picking up only “those aspects of a situation that are needed to accomplish the task at hand.”
5) So if you’re hitting a tennis ball, all you really need to focus on is your body & the ball.
6) When observing our own behavior, we must do so without judging it as positive or negative, right or wrong, good or bad.

Gallwey’s 4-Step Process:

1) Non-judgmental observation
2) Visualize the desired outcome
3) Trust Self 2
4) Non-judgmental observation of change and results.

Maybe wisdom is not so much to come up with new answers as to recognize at a deeper level the profundity of the age-old answers. - Timothy Gallwey

Reinforcement Learning

Share

Definitions

  • Agent: The RL algorithm that learns from trial and error
  • Environment: The world through which the agent moves
  • Action(A): All the possible steps that the agent can take
  • State(S): Current condition returned by the environment
  • Reward(R): An instant return from the environment to appraise the last action
  • Policy(π): The approach that the agent uses to determine the next action based on the current state
  • Value(V): The expected long-term return with discount, as opposed to the short-term reward R
  • Action-value(Q): This similar to Value, except, it takes an extra parameter, the current action (A)

    Concepts:

  • Reward maximization: Reward maximization theory states that, a RL agent must be trained in such a way that, he takes the best action so that the reward is maximum.
  • Exploratin & Exploitation: Exploitation is about using the already known exploited information to heighten the rewards. Exploration is about exploring and captureing more information about an environment.

    Markov Decision Process

    The mathematical approach for mapping a solution in reinforcement learning is called Markov Decision Process(MDP)
    The following parameters are used to attain a solution:
  • Set of actions, A
  • Set of states, S
  • Reward, R
  • Policy, π
  • Value, V
    Example : Find the shortest path between A and D with minumum possible cost.
    In this problem,
  • Set of states are denoted by nodes i.e.{A,B,C,D}
  • Action is to traverse from one node to another {A->B, C->D}
  • Reward is the cost represented by each edge
  • Policy is the path taken to reach the destination {A->C->D}

    Q - Learning

  • R-Matrix: The R-matrix represents the environment in which the agent will be operating, viewed in terms of the states which the agent can be in, the actions available to the agent from each state (which are generally viewed as moves to other states) and the rewards received for arriving at a state. The agent does not know the whole R-matrix; all it can see are the moves immediately available to it.
  • Q-Matrix:The Q-matrix represents the agent’s accumulated knowledge about its environment. It has the same structure as the R-matrix, but at the outset all the values in it are 0 (because it hasn’t learned anything yet). All it knows is that it wants to maximise its rewards.     Q - Learning Algorithm

    1. Set the gamma parameter, and environment rewards in matrix R
    2. Initialize matrix Q to zero
    3. Select a random initial state
    4. Set initial state = current state
    5. Select one among all possible actions for the current state
    6. Using this possible action, consider going to the next state
    7. Get maximum Q value for this next state based on all possible actions
    8. Compute: Q(state,action)= R(state,action) + Gamma * Max[Q(next state, all actions)]
    9. Repeat above steps until current state = goal state
